Baby Moses

Bebi Moses

This story is from Exodus chapter 1 verses 8-22 and chapter 2 verses 1-10

Dispela stori i stap long buk Kisim Bek sapta 1 ves 8-22, na sapta 2 ves 1-10.

A new Pharaoh ruled over Egypt where the Israelites were living.

Wanpela niupela king i bosim ol Isip, long ples wea ol lain Hibru i stap long en.

When he saw how many Israelites there were, he became afraid of their power.

Taim em i lukim ol lain Hibru i planti tru, na ol i stap strong, em i stat long poret olsem ol i gat paua long sanap strong long ol yet.

He warned his people that if war came, the Israelites might side with their enemies and then leave the country.

Em i givim tok lukaut long ol pipol bilong em olsem, "Sapos pait i kirap, ating bai ol Hibru i bung wantaim ol birua bilong yumi na pait long yumi, na ol bai i lusim kantri bilong yumi na ranawe."

So the Egyptians forced the Israelites to become their slaves.

Olsem na ol Isip lain i mekim tok strong, olsem ol lain Hibru mas i stap ol wok boi nating bilong ol.

Tough slave masters made them build the cities of Pithom and Rameses.

Ol makim ol strongpela na hatpela bosman, bilong mekim ol Hibru i wok hat tru.

Na ol i mekim ol Hibru i wokim tupela bikpela taun, Pitom na Rameses.

But despite their poor treatment, the Israelites grew stronger and more numerous.

Maski ol Hibru i stap long taim nogut wantaim ol lain Isip, ol i kamap planti lain manmeri tru na ol i stap strong.

Their Egyptian masters brutally forced them to work even harder.

Ol bosman bilong Isip i save tok strong na pusim ol Juda long wokhat nogut tru.

There were two midwives who helped the Hebrew women deliver their babies. Their names were Shiphrah and Puah.

Tupela nesmeri bilong Isip save helpim ol mama Hibru long karim bebi bilong ol. Nem bilong tupela, Sifra na Pua.

Pharaoh summoned the two midwives, and ordered them to kill any baby boys that were born to Hebrew women.  Only baby girls would be allowed to live.

Na king bilong Isip i singautim tupela i kam.​ Em i tokim tupela olsem, “Taim yutupela i helpim wanpela meri Hibru i karim pikinini, yu mas kilim ol bebi boi na larim ol pikinini meri i stap.”

But the midwives obeyed God rather than Pharaoh, and they let the baby boys live.

Tasol tupela meri harim tok bilong God, na ol i no bihainim tok bilong king. Ol larim ol bebi boi i stap laip tu, taim ol i bon.

Pharaoh again summoned the midwives to ask why the baby boys were not being killed. The excuse they gave was that the Hebrew women were giving birth to baby boys before they had time to get to them.

King i singautim tupela nesmeri kam gen na em i askim ol, “Bilong wanem yu no kilim ol bebi boi?” Na tupela mekim wanpela tok we king i bilipim ol. Ol i tok, “Ol mama Hibru i save karim ol pikinini boi hariap, bifo mipela kamap long helpim ol.”

Pharaoh gave orders to his people that every baby boy born to a Hebrew woman must be thrown in the River Nile.

King i givim oda i go long ol pipol bilong em olsem, “Sapos wanpela Hibru mama i karim wanpela bebi boi, ol i mas holimpas dispela pikinini na tromoi i go long wara Nail.”

When a Hebrew man and his wife from the tribe of Levi had a baby boy, they hid him away from the Egyptians.

Wanpela famili bilong Hibru, long lain Livai, i karim wanpela bebi boi, na ol i haitim em long ol lain Isip.

But as the baby grew older, his parents found it harder to keep him hidden.

Tasol taim pikinini i stat long gro na kamap bikpela, ol papa na mama i no inap moa long haitim em.

The baby’s mother came up with an idea to keep her baby safely out of sight. She found a basket made from papyrus leaves and covered it in tar and pitch to make it waterproof.

Olsem na mama i kamapim wanpela tingting, em mas haitim em, na nogat lain bai lukim em. Em i mekim wanpela basket wantaim lip papirus, na em banisim dispela lip wantaim strongpela wel, long wara noken go insait.

She put the baby in the basket and carried it down to the River Nile. Her young daughter Miriam helped her.

Na em i putim bebi i go insait long basket, na em i karim em i go long wara Nail. Yangpela pikinini meri bilong em, Miriam, i helpim em tu.

She hid the basket in the tall bulrushes by the side of the river. Miriam kept watch over her baby brother from a distance.

Em i haitim basket insait long ol pitpit bus long arere sait long wara, na Miriam i stap hait klostu, na em i was.

That day Pharaoh’s daughter came down to the river to bathe. She spotted the basket and sent one of her attendants to fetch it.

Long dispela dei, pikinini meri bilong king, i kam daun long wara Nail long waswas. Em i lukim dispela basket long pitpit, na em i salim wanpela hausmeri bilong em long kisim i kam long em.

When she opened the basket, she saw the baby and heard his helpless cries. She felt sorry for him. ‘This is one of the Hebrew babies,’ she said.

Taim em i opim basket, em i lukim dispela tarangu bebi i krai i stap. Em wari tru long em, na em i tok, “Dispela mas wanpela bilong ol bebi Hibru.”

Miriam, who had been watching, approached the princess. ‘Shall I go and get one of the Hebrew women to nurse the baby for you?’ she asked.

‘Yes,’ replied the princess.

Miriam, husat i was klostu, kirap na kam long prinses. “Inap mi kisim em i go long wanpela bilong mama Hibru long susuim bebi?” Miriam em i askim.

“Yes,” prinses i bekim tok.

Miriam ran to get her mother, and brought her to see the princess.

The princess said, ‘Take this baby and nurse him for me, and I will pay you.’

Miriam i ron i go kisim mama bilong em i kam long prinses. Na prinses i tok, “Kisim bebi i go long haus bilong yu, na lukautim em bilong mi, bai mi peim yu.”

So the mother looked after her own child. When he was older, she brought him to the princess, and the princess adopted the child as her son.

Olsem na mama i kisim bebi bilong em yet na lukautim. Taim em i gro na kamap bikpela, mama bilong em kisim em i go long prinses, na prinses i kisim em olsem wasmama bilong dispela pikinini man.

The princess named the child Moses, an Egyptian word meaning ‘drawn out’.

‘I drew him out of the water,’ she told everyone.

Prinses i putim nem bilong em, Moses. Long tokples Isip, em i tok, 'Rausim long wara'.

“Mi kisim em i kamaut long wara,” Prinses i tokim olgeta lain manmeri.
