Front Cover

A donkey speaks to Balaam

A donkii na koara bene Balaam

English
Bible
Picture on Left

Many years ago, there was a prophet called Balaam. He expected people to pay him for speaking the word of God.

Na teitei roho a peha poropita. ‌A henanae e Balaam. Eove na rake nana beori voni bari beve sue tavusu ni bona koara te Hunavaan.

Picture on Left

One day, Balek, the King of Moab, saw the Israelite people camped near his country. The people of Moab were terrified and said, “There are so many of these people. They will destroy everything and kill us all!”

So King Balek sent messengers to Balaam.

Teo peho boni, me Balek, e King va Moab, paa tara ribona ta Israel, eori he tea kemp rapana kantrii teve. Mea ta Moab paa naabu viravira vai roho rori. Meori paa boha voen, “A tavaan vai o bero kurusu. Eori pasi teki rori bona maamihu taba teara, bara to mene asun vamate vakavara araara.”

 

Me King Balek paa vaanao ri nao bona tau kiu teve vo te Balaam.

Picture on Left

King Balek’s messengers said to Balaam, “We will give you lots of money if you go and curse the Israelites. We believe that those you bless win battles, and those you curse are doomed.”

Mea tau kiu te Balek paa sue ki bene Balaam, “Enam pasi heu vuan amaa moonii amaa bero, bean nao tea kaesi hata ria ta Israel. Enam kou na vamanamana vo nomen, a tavaan vai to vaovoi ri noman, na oha rori bona maa puana, eori he to kaesi hata ri noman pasi tatekiu.”

Picture on Left

That night God spoke to Balaam in a dream. He said, “Don’t follow them! You must not curse the Israelites, for I have blessed them!”

Tea boni bona, me Hunavaan paa koara ki bene Balaam teo tatariopo. Mepaa boha voen, “Goe tea murina rii! Ean toro goe tea kaesi hata ria ta Israel, tea tabae, enaa na vaovoi ri roho ori.”

Picture on Left

Balaam obeyed God and sent the messengers away. They left, but after several days they returned, and said that they would give Balaam more money and that he would become famous.

Me Balaam paa murina bono moroko te Hunavaan, mepaa vaanao vahaa ribona tau kiu te King va Moab, meori paa nao. A meha maa boni he oha, meori paa vaakaku vaha bana vo nao te Balaam, meori paa boha voen, pasi hee vori bono moonii ​o beera, bara eve repaa gono bono hena beera.

Picture on Left

God spoke to Balaam again at night.  He said, “Ok, you can get up and go with these men, but you must only say what I tell you.”

Me Hunavaan paa koara vaha bana ki bene Balaam tea boni. Mepaa boha voen, “Bara, sun, ean re nao meria abana va rau. Evehee ​ean toro sue koa o moroko to pasi sue kiu naan.”

Picture on Left

The next morning Balaam rode his donkey and started going to Moab. But God was angry with Balaam because he was only thinking of the money he would get.

Tea nubunubu no meho boni, me Balaam paa hae bona donkii teve. Mepaa taneo tea nao vo Moab. Evehee Hunavaan na hevee ni bona, tea tabae, na upehe koana bono moonii to pasi gono nae.

Picture on Left

While Balaam was riding, his donkey suddenly saw the angel of the Lord standing in the road holding a sword. She ran off the road into a field, but Balaam beat her back onto the road.

E Balaam he tea hae bata bona donkii teve, mea donkii paa pahin tara koa bona enjol te Sunano, eve he tea sun hanana. Na ato ni nana bono rapisi puana. Mea donkii paa rosin kahi bona hanana vo rasuu. Evehee Balaam na rapisi bona, ore nao vahaa vo hanana.

Picture on Left

The angel then stood where the road went between two walls. The donkey tried to avoid the angel by walking close to the wall, but she crushed Balaam’s foot against the wall as she went past. So Balaam beat the donkey again. Then the angel blocked the path again and the donkey lay down.

Mea enjol paa no sun vaha bana hanana, varirovana ria bua taboo. Mea donkii paa butara tea kao geve kahi bona enjol, mepaa pita vahusu vo tea taboo. Evehee a moone Balaam na aha nie tea taboo, eve he tea oha bata. Me Balaam paa navuhu vaha bana bona donkii. Murinae, mea enjol paa sun pina vaha bana ni bona hanana, mea donkii paa goroho koa.

Picture on Left

What have I done . . .

Goe tea rapisi anaa!

In a fit of rage, Balaam beat the donkey a third time. Then the Lord caused the donkey to speak and she said, “What have I done that deserves you beating me three times?”

Me Balaam paa hevee viravira kurusu. Mepaa rapisi vaha bana bona donkii. ‌Me Sunano paa vaakokoara bona donkii, mepaa sue, ​​​“A tabae bene a hata to paa pakuu naa, mean asun vakukan nom anaa?”

Picture on Left

Balaam shouted in reply, “You’ve made me look like an idiot! If I had a sword with me, I’d kill you right now!” Then God opened Balaam’s eyes to see the angel who then said to him, “Go with the men, but say only what I tell you to say.”

Me Balaam paa koara vabeera, “Ean ge paa vaapote avanaa! Benaa tei me nom to rapisi puana, enaa pari asun vamateu vuan.”

Murinae, me Hunavaan paa mamata bona matane Balaam, mepaa tara bona enjol. Mea enjol paa sue ki bona, “Nao meria abana va rau, evehee ean toro sue koa a tabae to pasi sue kiu naan.”

Picture on Left

So Balaam went to King Balek and the king took him to a mountain where he could look down and see the Israelite army. Balaam offered sacrifices there then spoke the words that God gave him. Instead of cursing the Israelites, he blessed them.

Me Balaam paa nao te King Balek. Me King paa mee hiava bona tea voora vai to ante nae tea sun beve tara gunaha ri naona bono aamii va Israel. Me Balaam paa paku bona ​va-asi teebona, mepaa paku bono moroko to hee e Hunavaan bona. Eove saka kaesi hata haa ribona ta Israel. Ahiki. Eove na vaovoi vahoara ribari.

Picture on Left

King Balek was very angry. He took Balaam to another mountain to make more sacrifices. Again Balaam spoke the words God gave him and blessed the Israelites.

Me King Balek paa hevee viravira kurusu. Mepaa mee bene Balaam vo tea meha voora, eve re paku ta meha maa va-asi. Evehee Balaam na paku vakisi bono moroko to hee e Hunavaan bona. Na vaovoi vakisi ribona ta Israel.

Picture on Left

Then King Balek was furious and took Balaam to a third mountain to offer sacrifices. But the Spirit of God came on Balaam and he spoke only good things about the Israelites.

Me King Balek paa gavagava kurusu. Mepaa mee bene Balaam vo tea vakukan a voora, eve re paku ta meha maa va-asi. Evehee Balaam na hapusu ni bona Taun te Hunavaan, mepaa moroko koa ni bona maa taba matamataa to pasi tapaku kiri nana bona ta Israel.

Picture on Left

King Balek was full of rage, and shouted, “I called you to curse the Israelites and instead you have blessed them three times. Get out of here! Go back home!”

Me King Balek paa hata kurusu teo gavagava. Mepaa koara vabeera, “Enaa na dadao nao vuan, ean re ma kaesi hata ria ta Israel, evehee ean paa vaovoi rivori bono kukan o vuri. Kaokahi o hum vai! Ean toro voosu vahaa vaan tean.”

Picture on Left

Balaam replied, “The Israelites are as strong as lions. They will defeat your country in war.” Then he went back home.

He was not given the money or fame that King Balek had promised.

Me Balaam paa sue, ‌“A ta Israel na kikisi potee ri rori bono laion. Eori pasi ma ohau bona kantrii tean tea puana.” Murinae, mepaa voosu vahaa vaan teve.

Eori saka hee haa roho bari bono moonii ge o hena beera vai to sue varaba ki roho e King Balek bona.

Picture on Left

The story about Balaam is in Numbers 22-24.

O vahutate ne Balaam na tei nana te Nambas 22-24.
